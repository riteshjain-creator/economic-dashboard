🔴 FIFTH STRAIGHT LOSS
Oil Shock Triggers Defensive Rotation

📋 Today's Synopsis

Markets extended losses for the fifth consecutive week as Brent crude surged past $104 per barrel, triggering a sharp rotation into defensive sectors. The Nifty 50 fell 2.09% to close at 22,819, with small caps bearing the brunt of the selloff (-3.5%).
This isn't a healthy correction—it's a regime change. When oil spikes, volatility rises, and foreign investors dump emerging markets, you get what we saw today: broad-based capitulation with only defensive sectors (Pharma, FMCG) showing relative strength.
Nifty 50: 7-Day Performance
The chart tells the story: a steady grind lower with no meaningful bounce attempts. Each rally gets sold into. This is distribution, not consolidation.
💡 KEY TAKEAWAY
Until oil stabilizes below $100 or geopolitical tensions ease, expect continued volatility. Defensive sectors (Pharma, FMCG) are your only safe haven. Don't try to catch falling knives in cyclicals (Auto, Banks, Realty).

📚 HISTORICAL CONTEXT

Last time we saw this rotation pattern (February 2024), markets fell another 5% before bottoming. Defensive rotation is an early warning signal, not the bottom itself. When you see Auto/Banks/Realty start outperforming for 3+ consecutive days → that's your signal risk appetite is returning.

Crude Oil Spike: The Root Cause
Brent crude surged 4.2% to $104.49—the highest since October 2024. Every $10 increase adds ~0.4% to Indian inflation. If oil stays above $100, the RBI can't cut rates. No rate cuts = no liquidity boost = no market rally.
Who gets hit hardest? Airlines (fuel is 40% of costs) and Auto (consumer wallets squeezed). Last time oil breached $100 (2022), Nifty fell 8% over the next two months.
